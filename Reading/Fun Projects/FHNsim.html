How to Read the Phase Space

The Phase Portrait plots the fast voltage \(V\) (horizontal) against the slow recovery variable \(w\) (vertical). Instead of tracking time directly, it allows you to visualize the global mathematical trajectories of a spiking neuron.

  • The Nullclines represent the states where the rate of change is zero. The intersection of the Cubic \(V\)-nullcline and the Linear \(w\)-nullcline marks the equilibrium point.
  • Vector Field Arrows show the force and direction the state will move when released. Brighter/longer arrows indicate rapid motion (fast changes in \(V\)), typical of the action potential's rising phase.
  • Click anywhere inside the Phase Space to perturb the cell to a specific voltage/recovery state and witness the recovery cycle.

Key Neurobiology Dynamics to Observe

1. Excitability & Spiking Threshold: Load the Excitable Rest preset. Click the Pulse Stimulus button. If the voltage perturbation is small, the state slides back directly to rest. If it crosses the cubic nullcline's unstable middle branch (the threshold), it takes a massive orbital loop representing a full Action Potential spike before returning to rest.

2. The Hopf Bifurcation (Oscillation): Switch to the Tonic Spiking preset (raises \(I_{\text{ext}}\) to 0.50). Watch the linear nullcline intersect the cubic nullcline in its middle branch. The equilibrium point loses its stability, resulting in an attractive Limit Cycle where the neuron fires spikes indefinitely.

3. Excitation Block: Slide the External Current \(I_{\text{ext}}\) to extreme high values (e.g. \(> 1.5\)). The limit cycle disappears as the intersection shifts onto the stable right branch. The neuron is now "stuck" in a perpetually depolarized, inactive state.
