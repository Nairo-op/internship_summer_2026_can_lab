The Hodgkin-Huxley Model Equations

1. Complete Membrane Current Equilibrium

The total membrane potential $V$ of the neuron changes dynamically based on the applied experimental stimulation $I_{app}$ balanced against ionic currents flow:

$$C_m \frac{dV}{dt} = I_{app} - g_{Na} m^3 h (V - E_{Na}) - g_K n^4 (V - E_K) - g_L (V - E_L)$$

2. Gating Transitions ($n, m, h$)

Each gating variable representing gate opening probability evolves as:

$$\frac{dx}{dt} = \alpha_x(V)(1-x) - \beta_x(V)x$$

Where $x \in \{m, h, n\}$. It can also be written in terms of steady state $x_\infty(V)$ and activation time constant $\tau_x(V)$:

$$x_\infty(V) = \frac{\alpha_x}{\alpha_x + \beta_x}, \quad \tau_x(V) = \frac{1}{\alpha_x + \beta_x}$$

3. Dynamic Gates Conductances

  • $m$ (Activation - Sodium): Represents activation of Na+ channel (fast). Term is $m^3$.
  • $h$ (Inactivation - Sodium): Represents inactivation of Na+ channel (slow). Complete Na gate configuration is $m^3h$.
  • $n$ (Activation - Potassium): Represents activation of K+ channel (slower). Complete K gate configuration is $n^4$.

4. Kinetic Voltage Rates formulas

Transition rates ($\alpha$ and $\beta$) mapped to action potential voltage levels (with resting state at $-65\text{ mV}$):

Sodium Activation ($m$) $$\alpha_m(V) = \frac{0.1(V+40)}{1 - e^{-(V+40)/10}}$$ $$\beta_m(V) = 4 e^{-(V+65)/18}$$
Sodium Inactivation ($h$) $$\alpha_h(V) = 0.07 e^{-(V+65)/20}$$ $$\beta_h(V) = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-(V+35)/10}}$$
Potassium Activation ($n$) $$\alpha_n(V) = \frac{0.01(V+55)}{1 - e^{-(V+55)/10}}$$ $$\beta_n(V) = 0.125 e^{-(V+65)/80}$$
